Lolo National Forest — Fire Season Overview

The Lolo National Forest covers approximately 2.1 million acres in western Montana.
The primary drainages include the Bitterroot, Clark Fork, and Blackfoot rivers.

Fire weather: The forest experiences peak fire danger from July through September.
Fuel moisture typically drops below 10% in late July, creating critical conditions.

Key communities at risk: Missoula, Seeley Lake, Superior, Thompson Falls, Plains.

Historical fire data shows increasing frequency since 2000, with the largest fires
occurring during drought years with early snowmelt.
